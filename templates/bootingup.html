Lets Boot Up your Raspberry Pi!

You are almost there! You have got your first Raspberry Pi and have taken it out of the box. It look great! Doesn't it? You have got your case asssembled and all pirepheries are connected and you are keen as bean to turn it on. Well, go right ahead and turn it on by simply pluging it into power socket.

You will see text running up the screen - Yes, Raspberry Pi is alive ! What you see on the screen is CLI. Now you will understand why there was all this talk about Linux and CLI before hand.

You will see at the bottom of the screen login prompt raspberrypi login: and you will need to login into your Raspberry Pi

  • user: pi
  • password: raspberry

Note: when you are going to type in the password there will be nothing appering on the scree. This is Linux feature preventing people looking over your shoulder and at least seeing how long your password is.

Once you have logged in you should see command-line prompt like this pi@raspberrypi ~ $. Your prompt will change depending where abouts in the file system you are. Your prompt will change everytime you change directories e.g if you change into python_games/ directory by typing cd python_games/ and press enter, your promopt will now look like this pi@raspberrypi ~ /python_games $ .

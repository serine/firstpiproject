Lets Boot Up your Raspberry Pi!

You are almost there! So have got your first Raspberry Pi. You have taken it out of the box and it look great. You have got your case asssembled and all pirepheries are connected and you are keen to turn it on. Well, go right ahead and turn it on by simply pluging it into power socket.

You will see text running up the screen - Yes, Raspberry Pi is alive ! What you see on the screen is CLI. Now you will understand why there was all this talk about Linux and CLI before hand.

You will see at the bottom of the screen login prompt raspberrypi login: and you will need to login into your Raspberry Pi

  • user: pi
  • password: raspberry

Note: when you are going to type in the password there will be nothing appering on the scree. This is Linux feature preventing people looking over your shoulder and at least seeing how long your password is.

Once you have logged in you should see command-line prompt like this pi@raspberrypi ~ $. Your prompt ( pi@raspberrypi ~ $) will stay the same, but everything after $ will change depending on your actions. You will see as you change directries your prompt will reflect this e.g pi@raspberrypi ~ $ .
